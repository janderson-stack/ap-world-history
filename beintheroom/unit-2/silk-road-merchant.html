At the Caravanserai: Choices on the Silk Roads

It is the 13th century. You have arrived at a caravanserai outside Samarkand after weeks on the road. Your caravan carries silk, porcelain, and spices from eastern markets. Around you: merchants from a dozen different cultures, scholars passing through, guards looking for work, and a tax collector who wants a cut of everything. The Silk Roads are open — but every choice carries risk.

Historical Situation

You are entering a commercial crossroads in 13th-century Central Asia. The Mongol Empire has unified much of Eurasia and the Pax Mongolica makes long-distance trade safer than it has been in a century. But safer does not mean safe. Every decision on the road — whom to trust, which route to take, how to protect your goods — has consequences that will follow you all the way home.

Your task is to choose a historical role, make a key commercial decision, defend your reasoning with evidence, consider a tradeoff, respond to an AI coach, and then step out of character to write an AP-aligned reflection.

Profit vs. RiskLong-distance trade offers great rewards, but bandits, taxes, and unstable rulers can destroy a caravan before it reaches its destination.
Commerce vs. CultureThe roads carry ideas, religions, and diseases as easily as they carry silk. What you bring home may not just be goods.
Local vs. ConnectedThe more connected you are to distant markets, the more dependent you become on political stability you cannot control.

Step 2

Central Dilemma

Your caravan is stopped at Samarkand. You must decide how to proceed — how to protect your goods, build commercial relationships, and navigate the political landscape of the Silk Roads.

Each option has benefits and consequences. There is no simple correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and use historical evidence.

Extend Credit and Build Relationships

Use bills of exchange and banking house connections to extend credit, build merchant relationships, and invest in the network rather than maximizing a single trip.

Trade Directly for Maximum Profit

Trade directly without intermediaries, maximize profit on this journey, and avoid sharing revenue with brokers or escorts.

Pay for Full Mongol Protection

Pay the Mongol escort fee and travel the main route under Pax Mongolica protection, accepting the tax cost in exchange for security.

Divert to a Safer but Longer Route

Avoid the heavily taxed main route by taking a longer alternate road — lower cost, higher risk, no Mongol protection.
