After the Conquest: Life Under Mongol Rule

The Mongols have conquered your city. The initial destruction is over. Now, a Mongol administrator is in charge, and everyone in the city must figure out how to survive — and possibly thrive — under rulers who are foreign, militarily unstoppable, and surprisingly open to using local talent. The question is no longer whether to resist the conquest. The question is what to do now.

Historical Situation

You are navigating life in a city recently brought under Mongol control. The destruction of conquest is real — but so is the opportunity. The Mongols need local administrators, scholars, merchants, and religious figures to actually govern what they have conquered. Everyone in this scenario must decide how much to cooperate, what to protect, and what to use.

Your task is to choose a historical role, decide how to relate to Mongol power, defend your reasoning with evidence, consider a tradeoff, respond to an AI coach, and then step out of character to write an AP-aligned reflection.

Destruction vs. ConnectionThe Mongol conquests killed millions and destroyed cities. They also created the largest connected trade network in Afro-Eurasian history. Both things are true.
Collaboration vs. ResistanceServing the Mongol administration keeps you alive and potentially powerful. It also makes you complicit in their rule. Where do you draw the line?
Local vs. ImperialThe Mongols governed through local intermediaries who preserved some customs and destroyed others. Your identity and your survival may depend on which parts of your culture you protect.

Step 2

Central Dilemma

The Mongol administrator has summoned the city's leading figures. Everyone must declare how they will relate to the new power. What is your approach?

Each option has benefits and consequences. There is no simple correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and use historical evidence.

Integrate Actively into Mongol Administration

Actively seek positions in Mongol administration, adopt Mongol customs, and use imperial connections to accumulate influence and protect your interests.

Preserve Local Institutions from Within

Accept Mongol authority while working from within to protect local institutions — law, religion, scholarship, language — from being dismantled.

Use Pax Mongolica to Expand Commerce

Focus on commerce rather than governance. The Pax Mongolica opens trade routes that were previously impossible. Use them.

Maintain Cultural Distance While Cooperating Outwardly

Cooperate enough to avoid punishment while maintaining internal cultural loyalty, protecting traditions quietly, and waiting for political conditions to change.

AP Reflection Prompt

Your response should address at least one of the following: how the Mongols built and administered their empire (KC-3.2.I.B.iii), how Mongol expansion facilitated trade (KC-3.1.I.E.i), or the significance of the Mongol Empire in larger patterns of continuity and change.
