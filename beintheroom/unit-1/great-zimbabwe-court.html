The Great Zimbabwe Court

Great Zimbabwe is the most powerful state in southern Africa — a stone-walled city that controls the gold trade connecting the African interior to Swahili Coast merchants and the wider Indian Ocean world. The king rules through control of cattle, land, and gold. But rivals are emerging, trade relationships are shifting, and the court must decide how to maintain its dominance in a changing world.

Historical Situation

You are entering a policy debate inside the Great Zimbabwe court. The central question is not simply what would make the kingdom powerful. The harder question is what different people in Zimbabwe's society would consider wise, profitable, spiritually necessary, or dangerous.

Your task is to choose a historical role, recommend a policy, defend your reasoning with evidence, consider a tradeoff, respond to an AI coach, and then step out of character to write an AP-aligned reflection.

Gold vs. CattleGreat Zimbabwe's power rests on two foundations — controlling the gold trade with coastal merchants and controlling cattle herds that define wealth and status locally. Which matters more?
Coast vs. InteriorSwahili Coast merchants bring cloth, ceramics, and glass beads from across the Indian Ocean. But dependence on coastal trade creates vulnerability if those relationships break down.
King vs. RivalsRegional chiefs who once paid tribute are growing stronger. The king must decide whether to crush rivals, co-opt them, or share power.

Step 2

Central Dilemma

The king asks: How should Great Zimbabwe respond to growing regional rivals, shifting trade relationships, and the challenge of maintaining power in southern Africa?

Each option has benefits and consequences. There is no simple correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and use historical evidence.

Strengthen the Gold Trade

Invest in protecting and expanding trade routes to the Swahili Coast. Use Indian Ocean goods to reward loyal chiefs and bind them to Zimbabwe.

Crush Regional Rivals

Use military force to reassert dominance over regional chiefs who are growing too independent. Demonstrate that Zimbabwe's power cannot be challenged.

Invest in Cattle and Agricultural Wealth

Reduce dependence on the gold trade by building internal wealth through cattle herds and agricultural expansion. Create prosperity that does not depend on coastal merchants.

Build New Stone Enclosures

Invest in dramatic new stone construction to demonstrate Zimbabwe's power and permanence to rivals and foreign merchants alike.
