The Angkor Council

The year is c. 1200 CE. A new Khmer king has come to power at Angkor after defeating the Cham invaders who had sacked the capital. He faces a historic choice: should the kingdom continue its tradition of Hindu royal patronage — the devaraja cult that has legitimized Khmer kings for centuries — or should he shift state patronage to Buddhism, transforming the empire's religious identity and the institutions that depend on it? You have been summoned to advise the council.

Historical Situation

The Khmer king stands at a crossroads. For generations, the empire's legitimacy rested on the devaraja — the god-king tradition linking the ruler to Hindu cosmic order. Massive temples like Angkor Wat embodied this system. But Buddhist monasticism is growing in influence, and other rulers in Southeast Asia are turning toward the sangha. A shift in royal patronage would not simply change temple rituals — it would reshape who holds spiritual authority, how trade networks operate, and what the king owes to his subjects.

Your task: choose a historical role, recommend a path, defend it with evidence, consider a tradeoff, receive AI coaching, and then step out of character to write an AP-aligned reflection.

Religious tensionThe devaraja tradition connects royal power to Hindu cosmic order. Buddhism offers a different model of kingship — compassionate, reform-minded, popular.
Political tensionA shift in patronage creates winners and losers. Who gains spiritual authority? Who loses it? How does the king hold together a diverse empire?
Economic tensionTemple networks, merchant connections, and trade routes are all linked to religious identity. A change at the top reshapes trade relationships and tax flows.

Step 2

Central Dilemma

The king asks the council: What religious path should the Khmer kingdom follow to maintain legitimacy, stability, and power?

Each option has real benefits, real costs, and real opponents. There is no obvious correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and be supported by historical evidence.

Maintain Hindu Patronage

Continue the devaraja tradition. Build and maintain Hindu temples, support Brahmin priests, and preserve the ritual system that has legitimized Khmer kings for centuries.

Shift to Mahayana Buddhism

Embrace the Bodhisattva-king model. Build Buddhist temples and support the Mahayana sangha to demonstrate royal compassion and connect to growing Buddhist trade networks.

Adopt Theravada Buddhism

Follow the popular reform movement spreading from Sri Lanka. Theravada offers direct engagement with ordinary people, growing influence across mainland Southeast Asia, and a different model of royal virtue.

Pursue Syncretic Balance

Support both Hindu and Buddhist institutions. Maintain the devaraja cult for elite legitimacy while extending patronage to Buddhist monasteries to broaden popular support.
