Governing After Abbasid Fragmentation

The Abbasid Caliphate once united the Islamic world under one ruler. Now it has fragmented. New rulers — many of them Turkic military leaders — govern regions that were once under Abbasid control. You have been summoned to advise a new ruler on how to govern, build legitimacy, and hold a diverse society together without the authority of the caliph behind you.

Historical Situation

You are entering a policy debate in the court of a new ruler who rose to power after Abbasid fragmentation. The central question is not simply how to hold territory. The harder question is what different people in this society would consider legitimate, just, profitable, or dangerous.

Your task is to choose a historical role, recommend a policy, defend your reasoning with evidence, consider a tradeoff, respond to an AI coach, and then step out of character to write an AP-aligned reflection.

Military vs. LegitimacyYou hold power through force — but soldiers cannot govern alone. How do you earn the right to rule?
Center vs. LocalThe caliph's authority is gone. Local leaders, scholars, and merchants all want more autonomy. How much do you give?
Muslim vs. Non-MuslimYour territory includes Christians, Jews, and Zoroastrians. How you treat them shapes your legitimacy and your economy.

Central Dilemma

The court asks: What should the new ruler prioritize to establish legitimacy and hold this territory together?

Each option has benefits and consequences. There is no simple correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and use historical evidence.

Patronize Scholars and Translation

Fund Islamic scholarship, build libraries, and sponsor translations of Greek and Persian texts.

Protect Trade Routes

Invest in road security, caravanserais, and fair taxation for merchants.

Support Sufi Networks

Welcome Sufi teachers and their communities across your territory.

Build Legitimacy Through Islamic Law

Adopt and enforce sharia, appoint respected scholars as judges, and rule as a Muslim ruler.

Step Out of the Room: AP Reflection

Now leave the role and write as a historian. Explain what this debate reveals about governance and legitimacy in the Islamic world after Abbasid fragmentation.

AP Reflection Prompt
Using your role and the evidence you selected, explain how the fragmentation of the Abbasid Caliphate shaped both political authority and intellectual life in the Islamic world between c. 1200 and c. 1450. Your response should address at least one of the following: how new rulers established legitimacy (KC-3.2.I), or how Islamic states encouraged intellectual innovation and transfer (KC-3.2.II.A.i).
