Advising the Song Court

The Song dynasty is wealthy, urban, and highly commercialized. Its scholar-official government is admired, its cities are crowded with merchants and artisans, and its farms feed one of the largest populations in the world. But prosperity creates pressure. The emperor needs advice: where should the state place its energy if Song China is to remain stable, legitimate, and powerful?

Historical Situation

You are entering a policy debate inside the Song court. The central question is not simply what would make China powerful. The harder question is what different people in Song society would consider wise, moral, profitable, or dangerous.

Your task is to choose a historical role, recommend a policy, defend your reasoning, respond to coaching questions, and then step out of character to write an AP-aligned reflection.

2. Central Dilemma

The emperor asks: What should the Song state prioritize to strengthen the dynasty?

Each option has benefits and consequences. There is no simple correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and use historical evidence.

Expand Scholar-Official Government

Invest more deeply in civil service exams, Confucian education, and bureaucracy.

Support Trade and Commerce

Protect merchants, expand markets, support paper money, and strengthen canals.

Prioritize Agriculture and Stability

Protect farmers, expand rice production, maintain irrigation, and reduce disorder.
