Advising the Khmer King

Angkor is one of the most powerful cities in Southeast Asia. The Khmer king rules over a vast network of temples, irrigation canals, and trade routes. But the empire faces a choice — continue pouring resources into monumental temple construction to honor the gods and legitimize royal power, or invest in military defense, agriculture, and trade to maintain prosperity and security. You have been summoned to advise the king.

Historical Situation

You are entering a policy debate inside the Khmer court. The central question is not simply what would make Angkor powerful. The harder question is what different people in Khmer society would consider wise, moral, profitable, or dangerous.

Your task is to choose a historical role, recommend a policy, defend your reasoning with evidence, consider a tradeoff, respond to an AI coach, and then step out of character to write an AP-aligned reflection.

Temple vs. TreasuryTemple construction glorifies the king and honors the gods — but it drains resources that could feed armies and fund trade.
Hindu vs. BuddhistThe court is shifting from Hindu to Buddhist traditions. Which belief system should guide royal policy and justify the king's authority?
Center vs. FrontierAngkor's power depends on controlling distant regions. But the further the empire expands, the harder it becomes to hold together.

Step 2

Central Dilemma

The king asks: What should the Khmer king prioritize to maintain the empire's power and legitimacy?

Each option has benefits and consequences. There is no simple correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and use historical evidence.

Continue Temple Construction

Invest in a new temple complex to honor the gods, legitimize royal authority, and demonstrate Khmer power to neighboring kingdoms.

Strengthen Military Defenses

Redirect resources to the army, fortify borders, and send military forces to secure distant provinces against external threats.

Expand Agricultural Infrastructure

Invest in new irrigation canals and rice cultivation to support population growth, feed the army, and reduce dependence on forced labor.

Invest in Trade Networks

Protect and expand trade routes connecting Angkor to Indian Ocean commerce. Use merchant wealth to fund both temples and defense.
