A Feudal Manor in Crisis

It is the mid-14th century in Western Europe. The Black Death has swept through the region, killing a third of the population. Serfs are demanding better conditions or threatening to leave. Lords are struggling to maintain their estates without enough labor. The Church is under pressure as people question why God allowed such suffering. A meeting has been called on the manor to decide how to respond to the crisis.

Historical Situation

You are entering a crisis meeting on a medieval European manor. The central question is not simply how to survive the plague's aftermath. The harder question is what different people on the manor would see as just, practical, spiritually correct, or threatening to their way of life.

Your task is to choose a historical role, recommend a policy, defend your reasoning with evidence, consider a tradeoff, respond to an AI coach, and then step out of character to write an AP-aligned reflection.

Lord vs. SerfSerfs are the foundation of the manorial economy — but they know their labor is now scarce and valuable. The balance of power is shifting.
Church vs. CrisisThe Church has always provided spiritual comfort and social order. But the Black Death has shaken faith and created new questions about God, suffering, and authority.
Feudal Ties vs. FreedomThe feudal system depends on loyalty and obligation running in both directions. But when lords cannot protect their serfs from plague, what do those obligations mean?

Step 2

Central Dilemma

The lord asks: How should the manor respond to the labor crisis and social upheaval caused by the Black Death?

Each option has benefits and consequences. There is no simple correct answer. Your recommendation must fit your role and use historical evidence.

Maintain Feudal Order

Enforce existing feudal obligations. Prevent serfs from leaving. Use legal and physical authority to preserve the manorial system unchanged.

Offer Limited Wages and Concessions

Pay serfs small wages or reduce their labor obligations in exchange for continued work on the manor. Adapt to the new reality without fully abandoning feudal structure.

Rely on Church Authority

Work with the parish priest to restore order through spiritual authority. Frame acceptance of hardship as religious duty.

Grant Conditional Freedom

Allow serfs to leave serfdom in exchange for rent payments. Convert feudal obligations to cash relationships as the economy shifts.
