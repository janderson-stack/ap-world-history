A Note on Dates

This map shows the territory of the Northern Song Dynasty (960–1127 CE). In 1127, Jurchen forces from Manchuria captured the Song capital at Kaifeng, forcing the court to relocate south to Hangzhou. The resulting Southern Song Dynasty (1127–1279) governed a smaller territory south of the Huai River. The AP course framework dates for this topic (c. 1200–1450) fall primarily within the Southern Song period — but understanding the Northern Song's full territory helps explain how Song China developed the governance, culture, and economy you're studying.

Map Notes

Reading the Song World

This map shows East Asia during the Northern Song period. Notice that the Song did not dominate East Asia through conquest alone. It faced powerful neighbors to the north and west, which helps explain why administration, taxation, commercial infrastructure, diplomacy, and Confucian scholar-officials mattered so much.

The map is not just where Song China was. It shows the conditions that shaped Song policy.

Geographic Takeaway

The Song Dynasty had to administer, tax, trade, and negotiate its way to stability. Geography helps explain the importance of bureaucracy and commercial infrastructure in Topic 1.1.
