Map Notes

Reading the World of Islam

This map reveals the single most important geographic fact about Dar al-Islam: it was not one place controlled by one government. It was a network connected by shared faith, Arabic learning, legal traditions, scholarship, and trade routes across Afro-Eurasia.

Between 1200 and 1450, this world experienced political disruption and remarkable resilience. The Mongol invasion destroyed Baghdad in 1258, but Islamic networks survived because they had many centers of power, learning, and exchange.

Geographic Takeaway

Dar al-Islam held together less through unified military force than through shared faith, trade, scholarship, and legal-cultural networks.
